1) DECISION LOCK (HARD COMMITMENT)
- ACCEPTED DECISION (single, no alternatives):
  Launch a 60‑day operational plan to grow enrollment by improving funnel quality (content → lead → event → contract) with focus on conversion, CRM discipline, and event execution quality, without changing the product portfolio.
- REJECTED ALTERNATIVES (list):
  1) “Rely only on expensive direct high‑intent leads with low volume.”
  2) “Expand the product line before stabilizing conversion.”
  3) “Deep price discounting to chase volume.”
- OWNER CONFIRMATION:
  “I, __________________, accept this decision and take responsibility for execution.”

2) SHORT SUMMARY
- Problem in 1–2 sentences:
  The branch consistently misses enrollment targets and fails to launch new groups; within 2 months it must restore positive cash flow.
- Why this is urgent now:
  Current funnel quality and conversion levels keep the branch in a financial deficit.
- Target result (measurable):
  Within 2 months launch 6 adult groups (PL/EN/RU), +60 students, +50–60k PLN/month cash flow; within 1 month launch 3 adult groups (15 each) + 1 kids group.

3) PROBLEM STRUCTURE (MINIMUM)
- System boundary (inside/outside):
  Inside: local branch (marketing, sales, events, delivery, HR, local content/execution). Outside: central office (ad targeting and programs). Market/economy/regulation are external conditions but under the director’s managerial responsibility.
- Key actors and interactions:
  Branch Director → content maker → targeting (central office) → leads → sales team → events → contracts; instructors and training programs as part of the customer promise.
- Core contradiction:
  Cheap leads are low quality and don’t convert; expensive leads are high quality but too few and still not fully converted.

4) IDEAL FINAL RESULT (IFR)
- IFR statement:
  Within 2 months the branch launches 6 groups and gains +60 students with stable training quality and no product expansion.
- “No new harm” condition:
  Do not reduce training quality, do not dump price, and do not burn out the team.

5) EXECUTION PLAN (2–4 weeks)
- Workstream 1: Positioning and content
  Tasks:
  - Within 7 days define value messages by program (PL/EN/RU).
  - Rebuild creatives and event сценарии around target pains.
  - Run events with an external host or retrain the host.
- Workstream 2: Sales funnel and CRM discipline
  Tasks:
  - Enforce lead response SLA (time to first contact, number of touches).
  - Restart qualification scripts and follow‑up scenarios.
  - Weekly conversion audit: lead → confirmed → show‑up → contract.

6) METRICS & VERIFICATION
- Leading indicators:
  - Valid leads/week (target: +50% vs baseline).
  - Event show‑up rate (target: ≥30% of confirmations).
  - Event‑to‑contract conversion (target: ≥10%).
- Outcome metrics:
  - Groups launched (target: 3 by month 1; 6 by month 2).
  - New students (target: +60 in 2 months).
  - Cash flow (target: +50–60k PLN/month).
- Success criteria:
  Actual group launches and cash flow increase confirmed by the branch director.

7) COST OF ERROR (MANDATORY)
- Cost of non‑decision:
  Continued cash flow deficit and risk of branch closure/restructuring.
- Cost of rule violation:
  Budget wasted on low‑quality leads and loss of the 2‑month window.
- Cost of alternative path:
  Relying only on expensive direct leads yields insufficient volume to launch the required groups.

8) RULE (OPERATIONAL)
If for 2 consecutive weeks event‑to‑contract conversion < 10% and show‑up < 30%, then replace the event host and сценарий, and rebuild creatives with new messages.
Scope/exceptions:
Applies to all branch events regardless of audience language.
Rule owner: client (branch director).
